Playground

What to expect

  • No teams, no games, no competition: Playground is all about exploration, movement, and fun—not scoring goals or keeping standings.
  • Parent‑child participation: A parent or guardian partners with their child throughout the session, creating a supportive, bonding environment.
  • Activity‑based learning: Kids rotate through small “activity grids” designed to build coordination, balance, and confidence.
  • Flexible field setup: Sessions can run on a field, in a gym, on a tennis court—any open space works.

Session format

  • Length: Up to 60 minutes (including breaks)
  • Structure: A mix of guided activities, movement games, and playful challenges
  • Environment: Low‑pressure, supportive, and fun

Competition format

No scores, no standings, no referees. Children learn through discovery, imagination, and repetition—not competition.

  • Motor‑skill development
  • Social interaction
  • Confidence building
  • Enjoying movement and play

6U

Game format

  • Duration: Four 5-minute quarters
  • Halftime: 5-minute break
  • Players: 5v5 (no goalkeepers)
  • Ball: Size 3

Restarts and rules of play

  • Out-of-bounds: Pass-ins only (no throw-ins)
  • Free kicks: Direct free kicks only (no indirect)
  • Goal kicks: Not used in 6U
  • Corner kicks: Not used in 6U
  • Heading: Not allowed
  • Offside: Not enforced in 6U

Player participation

  • Minimum play: Each player must play at least 3 quarters
  • Typical playtime: Our region generally plays all players the full game
  • Substitutions: Only at quarter breaks

Coaches and referees

  • Referee: Home team provides the referee
  • Coach location: Coaches are not allowed on the field during play

Competition format

  • Scores: No scores are recorded
  • Standings: No standings are kept—focus is on fun and development

8U

Game format

  • Duration: Four 10‑minute quarters
  • Halftime: 5‑minute break
  • Players: 6v6 (with goalkeepers)
  • Ball: Size 3

Restarts and rules of play

  • Free kicks: Both direct and indirect free kicks are allowed
  • Goal kicks: Allowed
  • Corner kicks: Allowed
  • Throw‑ins: Used for all out‑of‑bounds restarts
  • Offside: Not enforced in 8U
  • Goalkeeper restrictions: No punting or drop‑kicks
  • Heading: Not allowed

Player participation

  • Minimum play: Each player must play at least 3 quarters
  • Goalkeeper rotation: Must change every quarter; no player may play goalkeeper for more than one quarter per game

Coaches and referees

  • Referee: Home team provides the referee
  • Coach location: Coaches are not allowed on the field during play

Competition format

  • Scores: No scores are recorded
  • Standings: No standings are kept — the focus is on development and fun
