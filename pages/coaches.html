About Coaching

AYSO coaching is built for everyday people - NO EXPERIENCE NEEDED - because our Region gives you all the training, tools, and support to grow into an amazing coach. You won’t be alone at any point; from age-appropriate AYSO courses to on-field guidance and a community of volunteers, we walk with you every step of the journey. Coaching becomes a chance to bond with your child, create joyful memories, and help kids develop confidence, teamwork, and a love of the game. AYSO’s nationally recognized training is designed specifically for volunteers with little or no soccer background, so if you bring enthusiasm and a willingness to learn, we can turn you into a certified, capable coach who truly brings out the best in kids.

Coach Conduct

  • POSITIVE COACHING - Bring encouragement, patience, and praise to every player. Help them grow, try new things, and feel proud of their effort.
  • BE PREPARED & ORGANIZED - Have a plan for each practice, bring the right equipment, and keep rosters and registration forms handy.
  • POSITIVE SIDELINE CONDUCT - Set the tone. Keep comments supportive, stay calm, and model the behavior we want our players and parents to follow.
  • RESPECT THE REFEREES - Our refs—youth and adult—deserve kindness and support. Their calls stand. We teach our players to play on, not argue.
  • ZERO TOLERANCE POLICY - No yelling at refs, no negative comments toward players, coaches, or parents. We protect the fun and safety of the game.
  • GOOD COMMUNICATION - Keep parents and your division coordinator in the loop about schedules, changes, and expectations. Clear communication makes the season smoother for everyone.
  • EXPECTATIONS FOR YOU, PLAYERS & PARENTS - Set simple, shared expectations early: effort, respect, teamwork, and positivity from all sides.
  • ALWAYS HAVE PLAYER FORMS - Bring player registration and emergency forms to every practice and game. Safety first.
  • GET YOUR PARENTS INVOLVED - Ask for help! Team parents, snack schedules, field setup, cleanup—AYSO works best when everyone pitches in.
  • REMEMBER: THE KIDS ARE HERE TO HAVE FUN! - Winning is optional. Joy, confidence, friendships, and a love for the game are the real goals.
