Team Roles & Expectations

Team Name Selection

Choose a fun, creative team name that isn’t tied to jersey color. This avoids confusion on game days and gives the kids something unique to rally around.

Assistant Coach

Each team is encouraged to have at least one assistant coach. Assistants complete the same certifications as the head coach to ensure consistency, safety, and support during practices and games.

Referee Support

Each team should identify 1–2 volunteers who can complete the 8U Referee certification or higher.

This is a huge help to the coach—teams referee their own games, so having trained parents or older siblings (12+) ready to step in keeps things running smoothly and lets the coach focus on the players.

Field Setup & Tear‑Down

  • First game of the day: Arrive one hour early to help with field setup (goals, cones, lines, etc.).
  • Last game of the day: Assist with full field tear‑down and equipment return.

There is no group after you, so the final team of the day is responsible for leaving their field completely cleared.

Snack Coordinator

The team parent organizes the snack schedule for halftime and post‑game treats. A simple rotation works well. Be sure to check for allergies or dietary restrictions before the first game.

Team Parties & End‑of‑Season Celebration

Many teams enjoy hosting a mid‑season get‑together or an end‑of‑season celebration. Options can be simple and low‑stress, such as:

  • A park meetup after the final game
  • A pizza party
  • A potluck or dessert party

The team parent typically coordinates dates, locations, and contributions so everyone feels included and the kids get a fun memory to wrap up the season.
