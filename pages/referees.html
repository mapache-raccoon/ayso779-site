Become an AYSO Referee

Referees are essential to the AYSO experience.

Referees are essential to the AYSO experience — and you don’t need prior soccer experience to get started. AYSO provides all the training you need, and many volunteers discover that refereeing is one of the most fun, rewarding ways to support their child and the community. Whether you’re a parent, a teen volunteer, or a returning referee, you’ll make a real impact every time you step on the field.

Referees help create a safe, fair, and positive environment where kids can grow, build confidence, and fall in love with the game. You’re supporting players, staying close to the action, and helping shape the kind of soccer experience that keeps kids coming back season after season.

Keeping the Game Safe, Fair, and Fun

Referees are at the heart of every AYSO game. We simply can’t play without them — but beyond necessity, the role is genuinely meaningful. You’re the steady presence who helps ensure that every match is safe, respectful, and development‑focused.

Why Become an AYSO Referee?

We Need You

Every game requires a referee. When parents and community members step into this role, it keeps our program running smoothly and ensures every team gets the chance to play.

It’s Fun and Incredibly Rewarding

Working with kids, watching them grow, and being part of their soccer journey is one of the best parts of volunteering. Many referees find they enjoy it even more than they expected.

You’re the Guardian of the Game

Referees are the independent officials who manage the match. Your authority extends to:

  • Players
  • Substitutes
  • Coaches and team officials
  • Spectators
  • Assistant referees

Your presence helps maintain a positive, respectful environment where kids can focus on playing and learning.

Safety Comes First

The referee’s top priority is player safety. While all sports carry some risk, referees help minimize it by monitoring:

  • Field conditions
  • Equipment
  • Player behavior
  • Spectator conduct

A safe game is a fun game — and kids stay in the sport longer when they feel protected and supported.

Fairness and Flow

Referees enforce the Laws of the Game in a way that keeps play:

  • Safe
  • Fair
  • Fun

Good referees interfere as little as possible, calling only what they clearly see and avoiding “doubtful or trifling” offenses. This helps the game flow and keeps the focus on the players.

Understanding What “Fun” Looks Like

Fun looks different at each age group. Referees learn how to adjust their approach so the game stays development‑appropriate — from the joyful chaos of 6U to the more structured play of older divisions.

Steps to Becoming an AYSO Referee

  1. Register as an AYSO Volunteer
    • Go to ayso779.org
    • Log in, or click Register Now to create an account
    • Click Volunteer in the left menu
    • Select Find Volunteer Roles
    • Under Volunteer Registration, click + Select → View Roles
    • Choose Referee (and any other roles you’d like)
    • Complete the E‑Signature process
    • Follow the prompts to finish your account setup

    Note: Access to AYSOU (the training platform) becomes available the next morning.

  2. Complete All Safe Haven Courses

    All volunteers must complete:

    • AYSO’s Safe Haven
    • CDC Concussion Awareness
    • Sudden Cardiac Arrest
    • SafeSport (required annually for volunteers age 18+)

    How to access courses:

    • Log in to My Account → Volunteer → click AYSOU
    • Go to Training Library → Safe Haven Courses
    • Complete all modules
    • Save or print your certificates
  3. Complete Referee Training

    You can complete your referee certification in one of two ways:

    Option A: Online + In‑Person (Most Common)

    Regional Referee Online Companion Course

    Step 1 — Register for the Classroom Session

    • Log in to AYSOU
    • Go to In‑Person Courses → Referee Instructor Led Courses
    • Select Regional Referee Online Companion Course
    • Click Sessions and register for a date

    Step 2 — Complete the Online Module

    • Go to Online Courses → Regional Referee Training Course
    • Complete the online portion before attending the class

    Step 3 — Attend the Classroom Session

    • Attend the in‑person session you registered for
    • Bring your Safe Haven and Concussion certificates

    Option B: Full In‑Person Regional Referee Course

    An 8‑hour course offered in Chino Hills and nearby regions.

    • Log in to AYSOU
    • Go to Training Event
    • Search for Regional Referee – Full In‑Person Course
    • Choose a date and location, enroll, and attend

Frequently Asked Questions

Is there an age requirement?

Yes. Referees must be at least 12 years old. Youth referees may officiate games one age division below their own age.

Do I need to pre‑register for a course?

Yes — you must register in AYSOU to receive credit.

What should I bring to the course?

  • Safe Haven, Concussion, and Regional Referee certificates
  • Pen/pencil and paper
  • All course materials are provided

What if I arrive late?

Punctuality is important. Late arrivals may be asked to attend another session.

I’m a USSF referee. Can I referee AYSO games?

Yes — after completing the USSF‑AYSO crossover process. Link to crossover form/resource
